What is the Tragedy of the Commons?

The Tragedy of the Commons is a concept first introduced by ecologist Garrett Hardin in 1968. It describes how individuals, acting in their own self-interest, can deplete shared resources, leading to negative consequences for everyone.

Imagine a beautiful **village garden** that belongs to everyone. Initially, it thrives as a shared resource. But as villagers take more than their fair share, the garden begins to suffer, becoming barren and unusable. This simple story illustrates a bigger issue that affects **our environment, economy, and society** today.

Key Concepts:

  • When a resource is shared by many but owned by none, conservation is often neglected.
  • Short-term individual gain can lead to long-term collective loss.
  • Regulation and communal responsibility are needed to prevent resource depletion.
